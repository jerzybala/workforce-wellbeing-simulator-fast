Workforce Wellbeing & Productivity Simulator

User Guide  ·  Version 2026-02-25

What is this tool?

The Workforce Simulator lets you explore how work environment and lifestyle factors affect two important outcomes for your team: mental health and workplace productivity.

You move sliders to describe conditions — things like how much recognition people receive, how often they exercise, what they eat — and the simulator instantly shows you the predicted impact on mental health and productive days.

The simulator is powered by machine learning models trained on large-scale workforce survey data. It is a what-if exploration tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Use it to compare scenarios, identify priority areas, and build the case for workplace interventions.

Two ways to use it
Individual mode — explore how changes affect one person's scores. Great for understanding the model and testing scenarios.

Team mode — upload a CSV with your team's survey responses. The simulator uses your real team data as a baseline and shows how uniform interventions would move the whole team's outcomes.

Understanding the scores

Capacity to Perform
0–100 scale. Higher is better. Reflects overall mental wellbeing based on work and lifestyle conditions. Shown as change from baseline.
Productive Days (per month)
Shows the baseline productive days at rest, plus the change when you adjust sliders. Positive delta = more productive days gained.
TeamQ — Team composite index
0–100 score reflecting your team's overall conditions. The displayed number is the average of the Wellbeing index (weighted by MHQ importance) and the Productivity index (weighted by productive-days importance). 100 means all factors are at their best; 0 means all are at their worst. Only visible in Team mode after CSV upload.

Capacity to Perform

The blue card shows the Mental Health Quotient (MHQ) — a 0–100 score where higher is better. It is displayed as a change from your baseline: +5.2 means conditions have improved enough to predict a 5.2 point increase in mental wellbeing.

Productive Days (per month)

The green card shows two numbers:

  • Baseline productive days — the estimated number of fully productive working days per month at baseline conditions (calculated as 22 working days minus the model's predicted unproductive days).
  • Change from sliders — how many additional productive days the simulated conditions would recover (or lose). A positive number, e.g. +0.8, means nearly one full extra productive day per person per month.

The card turns amber when the model predicts more than 3 unproductive days per month under the current conditions.

TeamQ Team mode only

TeamQ only appears after you upload a team CSV. It gives you an at-a-glance composite summary of your team's overall conditions on a 0–100 scale.

The score shown is the average of two underlying indices:

  • Wellbeing index — weights each factor by its importance to the MHQ model. Factors like Pride & Purpose, Relationship with Colleagues, and Diet carry the most weight.
  • Productivity index — weights each factor by its importance to the productive days model. Factors like Pride & Purpose, Reward & Recognition, and Work-Life Balance carry the most weight.

As you move sliders, the TeamQ score updates live. The delta shown ("vs baseline") indicates how much the score has shifted from the team's actual uploaded conditions.

The 11 input factors

All factors come from a workforce wellbeing survey. Each slider in the app corresponds to one of these factors.

Work environment factors (rated 1–9)

Each factor is rated on a 1 to 9 scale: 1 = very negative experience, 9 = very positive experience.

FactorWhat it measures
Work-Life BalanceHow much flexibility and control you have over your time (e.g. leaving work for personal matters)
Job SecurityHow safe and secure you feel in your role
Manageable WorkloadWhether the volume of work feels manageable
Pride & PurposeWhether your work feels meaningful and worthwhile
Learning & GrowthOpportunities to grow and develop your skills
Relationship with ColleaguesQuality of social relationships and support from co-workers
Relationship with ManagerQuality of your relationship with your direct manager
Flow of InformationWhether you feel well-informed about what is happening in your team
Reward & RecognitionWhether you feel valued and appreciated for your contributions

Lifestyle factors

These are ordinal categories rather than a numeric rating:

FactorScaleOptions (1 = worst, highest = best)
Exercise 1–5 1 Rarely/Never → 2 Less than once a week → 3 Once a week → 4 Few days a week → 5 Every day
Diet 1–5 1 Several times a day (ultra-processed) → 2 Once a day → 3 Few days a week → 4 A few times a month → 5 Rarely/Never
Which factors matter most?
For mental health (Capacity to Perform), the top drivers are: Pride & Purpose, Relationship with Colleagues, Reward & Recognition, Exercise, and Diet.

For productive days, the top drivers are: Pride & Purpose, Reward & Recognition, Work-Life Balance, Relationship with Colleagues, and Exercise.

Pride & Purpose and Reward & Recognition feature prominently in both lists — improving them has the broadest positive impact.

Individual mode Individual

Use individual mode to explore scenarios for a single person.

1
Set the sliders to reflect current conditions.
Move each slider to match the person's actual situation — their exercise habits, diet, workplace experience, and so on.
2
Click "Set Baseline".
This saves the current slider positions as your reference point. An orange tick mark appears on each slider to show the baseline position. The Capacity to Perform and Productive Days cards will now show the baseline values.
3
Move sliders to simulate a change.
For example, increase Reward & Recognition from 4 to 8. The score cards update instantly to show how much Capacity to Perform and productive days would change compared to your baseline. The orange tick marks show how far you've moved from baseline.
4
Use Optimization or Sensitivity to find the best levers.
Not sure where to focus? Click Productivity, MHQ, or Balanced to let the simulator find the best 2 factors to improve. Or click Sensitivity to see how each factor affects outcomes across its full range.
Tip: Click "Set Baseline" again (it becomes "Clear Baseline") to clear the reference point and start fresh. "Reset Defaults" returns all sliders to their default middle values without clearing the baseline.

Team mode Team

Team mode uses your team's real survey data as the baseline.

1
Switch to Team mode using the toggle at the top of the page.
2
Upload your team CSV.
The file must have one row per team member and columns matching the 11 survey factors. See CSV format below. After upload, the sliders are set to your team's actual averages, the TeamQ score appears, and orange tick marks on sliders show the team baseline position.
3
Move sliders to simulate interventions.
Each slider represents a uniform shift applied to every team member. For example, moving Reward & Recognition from 5 to 7 simulates what would happen if every team member's recognition score improved. Orange tick marks show where each factor started.
4
Read the results.
The Capacity to Perform and Productive Days cards show average changes across the whole team. The TeamQ score updates to reflect the new simulated conditions.
5
Generate a report.
Click "Generate Report" to download a PDF summary of the team's baseline profile and key recommendations.
Max All: The "Max All" button sets every slider to its maximum value — useful for seeing the theoretical best-case outcome for your team if all factors were improved to their highest levels.

Moments: If your CSV includes optional "Moments" columns (e.g. Goal Alignment, Team Meetings, Feedback), click the "Moments" button to view a ranked bar chart of team averages for those items.

Optimization

The optimizer answers the question: "Given that I can only change a few things, which ones will make the biggest difference?"

Before running optimization, you need a baseline set (individual mode) or team data loaded (team mode). Then select how many factors to change (1–4, default 2) and click one of the three goals:

GoalWhat it maximises
ProductivityReduction in unproductive days (more productive days gained)
MHQImprovement in mental health / Capacity to Perform score
BalancedBoth outcomes (weighted 60% MHQ, 40% productivity)

After running, the simulator highlights the recommended factors in green on the sliders and moves them to the suggested values automatically so you can see the effect immediately.

How does it work? The optimizer tests all possible combinations of the chosen number of factors, evaluating every meaningful level for each one. It picks the combination with the highest predicted improvement. With k=2 and 11 factors this is 55 combinations — fast and exhaustive.

Sensitivity analysis

Sensitivity analysis answers: "What would happen to outcomes if I pushed each factor from its current level all the way to its maximum?"

Click the Sensitivity button (requires a baseline or team data) to open a ranked list of all 11 factors. The panel opens on the Productive Days tab by default. For each factor you see:

  • A sparkline curve showing how outcomes change as the factor moves from its current value to its maximum
  • The slope — how many productive days (or MHQ points) you'd gain per unit increase

Use the Productive Days / MHQ toggle at the top of the panel to switch which outcome is used for ranking and curve display.

Sensitivity is most useful for understanding which factors have room to grow given current conditions. A factor already at a high level won't show much remaining headroom even if it's inherently important.

Model source

The "Model Source" dropdown in the sidebar lets you choose which trained model to use for predictions. The models differ in which survey population they were trained on:

ModelWho it represents
Western Countries (default)Western Europe, USA, Canada, Australia — approx. 5,800 respondents
Western Age >45Same countries, respondents over 45
Western Age ≤45Same countries, respondents 45 and under
All DataGlobal dataset — approx. 32,000 respondents

For most teams in Western Europe or North America, the default Western Countries model is the most appropriate choice. If your team is predominantly older or younger, the age-segmented models may give more accurate predictions.

CSV format for team upload

Your CSV should have one row per team member and one column per factor. The maximum size is 10,000 rows.

Column headers can be either the survey question text (CEN format) or the short feature names below. The required columns are:

Feature nameApp labelValid values
work_control_timeWork-Life Balance1–9
work_control_jobJob Security1–9
work_amountManageable Workload1–9
work_purposePride & Purpose1–9
work_learningLearning & Growth1–9
work_colleaguesRelationship with Colleagues1–9
work_managerRelationship with Manager1–9
work_informedFlow of Information1–9
work_recognitionReward & Recognition1–9
exercise_freq_ordExercise1–5 or text label
UPF_freq_ordDiet1–5 or text label
Missing values: Rows with any missing values in the required columns are dropped automatically. A warning will tell you how many rows were removed. Aim for complete data for the most accurate team representation.
Optional Moments columns
If your CSV includes any of the following columns, the app will compute their team averages and display them as a ranked bar chart (click "Moments" after upload):

Goal Alignment • Team Onboarding • Team Meetings • Professional Development Conversations • Feedback • After-Action Reviews • Conflict • Celebration and Recognition • Team Social Connection • Personal Circumstances (Joy or Adversity) • Failure • Leaving the Team • Handovers
