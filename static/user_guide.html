Workforce Wellbeing & Productivity Simulator

User Guide  ·  Version 2026-02-24

What is this tool?

The Workforce Simulator lets you explore how work environment and lifestyle factors affect two important outcomes for your team: mental health and workplace productivity.

You move sliders to describe conditions — things like how much recognition people receive, how often they exercise, what they eat — and the simulator instantly shows you the predicted impact on mental health and productive days.

The simulator is powered by machine learning models trained on large-scale workforce survey data. It is a what-if exploration tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Use it to compare scenarios, identify priority areas, and build the case for workplace interventions.

Two ways to use it
Individual mode — explore how changes affect one person's scores. Great for understanding the model and testing scenarios.

Team mode — upload a CSV with your team's survey responses. The simulator uses your real team data as a baseline and shows how uniform interventions would move the whole team's outcomes.

Understanding the scores

Mental Health Quotient (MHQ)
0–100 scale. Higher is better. Reflects overall mental wellbeing based on work and lifestyle conditions.
Productive Days (per month)
Shows the change in days lost to low productivity. Positive = more productive days gained.
TeamQ — Wellbeing index
0–100 composite score reflecting your team's overall wellbeing conditions. Weighted by what matters most for mental health.
TeamP — Productivity index
0–100 composite score reflecting your team's productivity conditions. Weighted by what matters most for reducing unproductive days.

Mental Health Quotient (MHQ)

MHQ is a 0–100 score where higher is better. It is shown as a change from your baseline — so +5.2 means conditions have improved enough to predict a 5.2 point increase in mental health scores.

In team mode the blue card shows the average MHQ change across all team members, with a range showing the spread from the person with the smallest gain to the largest.

Productive Days (per month)

The green card shows the predicted change in productive working days per month. A positive number (e.g. +0.8 days) means the simulated conditions would be expected to recover nearly one full productive day per person per month compared to the baseline.

The card turns amber when the model predicts more than 3 unproductive days per month under the current conditions — a flag worth paying attention to.

TeamQ and TeamP Team mode only

These two scores only appear after you upload a team CSV. They give you an at-a-glance summary of your team's overall conditions on a 0–100 scale — 100 means every factor is at its best possible level, 0 means every factor is at its worst.

Why two scores? The factors that most influence mental health are not identical to those that most influence productivity:

  • TeamQ (purple) weights factors by their importance to mental health — purpose at work, colleagues support, and diet carry the most weight.
  • TeamP (teal) weights factors by their importance to productivity — purpose at work, recognition, and control of time carry the most weight.

At a typical baseline the two scores are very close. They diverge noticeably when you adjust factors like Control of Time (much more important for productivity) or Ultra-processed Food (much more important for mental health).

As you move sliders, both scores update live to reflect the current simulated conditions.

The 11 input factors

All factors come from a workforce wellbeing survey. Each slider in the app corresponds to one of these factors.

Work environment factors (rated 1–9)

Each factor is rated on a 1 to 9 scale: 1 = very negative experience, 9 = very positive experience.

FactorWhat it measures
Control of TimeHow much flexibility and control you have over when and how you work
Job SecurityHow safe and secure you feel in your role
Work AmountWhether the volume of work feels manageable
Purpose at WorkWhether your work feels meaningful and worthwhile
Learning at WorkOpportunities to grow and develop your skills
Colleagues SupportQuality of relationships and support from co-workers
Manager SupportQuality of your relationship with your direct manager
Feeling InformedWhether you feel in the loop about what is happening in your team
RecognitionWhether you feel valued and appreciated for your contributions

Lifestyle factors

These are ordinal categories rather than a numeric rating:

FactorScaleOptions (1 = worst, highest = best)
Exercise Frequency 1–5 1 Rarely/Never → 2 Less than once a week → 3 Once a week → 4 Few days a week → 5 Every day
Ultra-processed Food 1–5 1 Several times a day → 2 Once a day → 3 Few days a week → 4 A few times a month → 5 Rarely/Never
Which factors matter most?
For mental health (MHQ), the top drivers are: Purpose at work, Colleagues support, Recognition, Exercise frequency, and Ultra-processed Food.

For productivity, the top drivers are: Purpose at work, Recognition, Control of time, Colleagues support, and Exercise frequency.

Purpose at work and recognition feature prominently in both lists — improving them has the broadest positive impact.

Individual mode Individual

Use individual mode to explore scenarios for a single person.

1
Set the sliders to reflect current conditions.
Move each slider to match the person's actual situation — their sleep quality, exercise habits, workplace experience, and so on.
2
Click "Set Baseline".
This saves the current slider positions as your reference point. The MHQ and Productive Days cards will now show 0 (no change yet).
3
Move sliders to simulate a change.
For example, increase Recognition from 4 to 8. The score cards update instantly to show how much MHQ and productive days would change compared to your baseline.
4
Use Optimization or Sensitivity to find the best levers.
Not sure where to focus? Click MHQ, Productivity, or Balanced to let the simulator find the best 2 factors to improve. Or click Sensitivity to see how each factor affects outcomes across its full range.
Tip: Click "Set Baseline" again to clear the baseline and start fresh. "Reset Defaults" returns all sliders to their default middle values without clearing the baseline.

Team mode Team

Team mode uses your team's real survey data as the baseline.

1
Switch to Team mode using the toggle at the top of the page.
2
Upload your team CSV.
The file must have one row per team member and columns matching the 12 survey factors. See CSV format below. After upload, the sliders are set to your team's actual averages, and TeamQ/TeamP scores appear.
3
Move sliders to simulate interventions.
Each slider now represents a uniform shift applied to every team member. For example, moving Recognition from 5 to 7 simulates what would happen if every team member's recognition score improved by the same relative amount.
4
Read the results.
The MHQ and Productive Days cards show average changes across the whole team, plus the range from the person benefiting least to most. TeamQ and TeamP update to reflect the new simulated conditions.
5
Generate a report.
Click "Generate Report" to download a PDF summary of the team's baseline profile and key recommendations.
Max All: The "Max All" button sets every slider to its maximum value — useful for seeing the theoretical best-case outcome for your team if all factors were improved to their highest levels.

Optimization

The optimizer answers the question: "Given that I can only change a few things, which ones will make the biggest difference?"

Before running optimization, you need a baseline set (individual mode) or team data loaded (team mode). Then select how many factors to change (1–4, default 2) and click one of the three goals:

GoalWhat it maximises
MHQImprovement in mental health score
ProductivityReduction in unproductive days
BalancedBoth (weighted 60% MHQ, 40% productivity)

After running, the simulator highlights the recommended factors in green on the sliders and shows the predicted improvement. The sliders move to the suggested values automatically so you can see the effect immediately.

How does it work? The optimizer tests all possible combinations of the chosen number of factors, evaluating every meaningful level for each one. It picks the combination with the highest predicted improvement. With k=2 and 12 factors this is 66 combinations — fast and exhaustive.

Sensitivity analysis

Sensitivity analysis answers: "What would happen to outcomes if I pushed each factor from its current level all the way to its maximum?"

Click the Sensitivity button (requires a baseline or team data) to open a ranked list of all 12 factors. For each factor you see:

  • A sparkline curve showing how outcomes change as the factor moves from its current value to its maximum
  • The slope — how many MHQ points (or productive days) you'd gain per unit increase
  • The total gain — the full change from current to maximum

Use the MHQ / Productive Days toggle at the top of the panel to switch which outcome is used for ranking and curve display.

Sensitivity is most useful for understanding which factors have room to grow given current conditions. A factor already at a high level won't show much remaining headroom even if it's inherently important.

Model source

The "Model Source" dropdown in the sidebar lets you choose which trained model to use for predictions. The models differ in which survey population they were trained on:

ModelWho it represents
Western Countries (default)Western Europe, USA, Canada, Australia — approx. 5,800 respondents
Western Age >45Same countries, respondents over 45
Western Age ≤45Same countries, respondents 45 and under
All DataGlobal dataset — approx. 32,000 respondents

For most teams in Western Europe or North America, the default Western Countries model is the most appropriate choice. If your team is predominantly older or younger, the age-segmented models may give more accurate predictions.

CSV format for team upload

Your CSV should have one row per team member and one column per factor. The maximum size is 10,000 rows.

Column headers can be either the survey question text or the short feature names. The required columns are:

Missing values: Rows with any missing values in the required columns are dropped automatically. A warning will tell you how many rows were removed. Aim for complete data for the most accurate team representation.
